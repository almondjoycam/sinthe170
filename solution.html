What approaches can we take?

Harm reduction is an approach to drug use that aims to give substance users autonomy to live healthily.

This can be considered the opposite of punitive drug policy, which criminalizes drug possession and use, and pushes substance users into a repeating cycle of incarceration and withdrawal.

Legal Regulation and Safer Supply

The Drug Policy Alliance, a US organization, is exploring legal regulation and safer supply. These are the main potential benefits of this approach.

Increased transparency in ingredient listing.
Unregulated and illegal drug suppliers often sell mixed or impure products. Safer supply of drug manufacturing could prevent impurities or unknown additives in products. Reducing the potential of drug additives also reduces the risk of serious or fatal drug interactions.
Stricter marketing rules.
Sometimes, vendors falsely advertise their products as a substance they are not. Legal regulation of a drug distribution market could significantly reduce false advertising.

If a drug is legally regulated, it is bound to the Code of Federal Regulations (CFR). Title 21 of the CFR, section 201.10 details federal regulations on listing the ingredients of a drug prominently on the label, without any misleading information.
